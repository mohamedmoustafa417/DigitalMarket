ChatGPT for Small Business — the Practical Playbook

DigitalMarket · Updated 2026-06-11 · All articles

ChatGPT is free, speaks Arabic fluently, and never sleeps — yet most small business owners use 5% of it, asking generic questions and getting generic answers. The difference between toy and tool is the prompt.

The anatomy of a working prompt

Compare: "Write an Instagram post about my shop" versus "You are a social media copywriter. Write 3 Instagram captions in Egyptian Arabic for a home-bakery launching cheesecake boxes, audience: working women 25–40 in Cairo, tone: warm and playful, each under 30 words with a CTA to order on WhatsApp." Role + context + audience + constraints + output format. That structure is repeatable for every department of your business.

Where the hours actually come back

  • Marketing: a month of post ideas, captions in masry, ad copy variants — 30 minutes instead of a weekend.
  • Customer service: polite replies to complaints, refund messages, FAQ answers — consistent tone even on stressful days.
  • Sales: price-increase emails, follow-ups, WhatsApp catalogue descriptions.
  • Operations: job descriptions, interview questions, supplier negotiation drafts, meeting summaries.
  • Finance: invoice reminder sequences, simple cash-flow explanations of your own numbers.

Three rules that keep AI safe for business

  1. Never paste secrets — customer data, supplier prices, anything you'd hide from a competitor.
  2. AI drafts, you decide — review every output; you know your market, it doesn't.
  3. Save winning prompts — a prompt that worked once is a reusable asset. That's literally what a prompt library is.
